About Deepwater Design

Built on precision. Focused on trades.

Not your average web designer

I'm Kieran. Before I built websites, I served in the British Army as an Electronic Warfare & Signals Intelligence Operator, posted to an EOD unit as an Electronic Countermeasures (ECM) Operator. That background demands precision, reliability, and getting the job done right first time. I bring the same discipline to every website I build.

Most web designers produce websites that look fine in a browser preview and fall apart in the real world: slow to load, hard to find on Google, and built on platforms that charge you monthly just to stay online.

I do things differently. Every site is hand-coded from a blank file. No templates, no drag-and-drop builders, no frameworks doing the heavy lifting. Just clean, purposeful code that performs.

The result is a website that loads fast, ranks better, and gives customers a reason to call you instead of the next result on Google.

I focus on UK trades and construction businesses because that's where I can make the biggest difference. Most trade businesses have either no website or a dated template that's doing them more harm than good. I fix that. Fixed price, fixed timeframe, no lock-in.

My focus

Why I focus on trades businesses

The trades industry has a web design problem. Most businesses fall into one of three situations.

  • No website at all

    Relying entirely on word of mouth. It works until it doesn't. When a customer searches online and can't find you, they call the next business on the list.

  • A dated template site

    Looks amateur, loads slowly, and costs a monthly fee just to stay online. You don't own it. Stop paying and it disappears. It is doing more harm than good.

  • An overpriced agency build

    Six months to deliver, three times the budget. You end up with a bloated WordPress site you can't touch without going back to the agency and paying again.

There is a middle ground: a properly built, professionally designed website at a fixed price, delivered in 14 days, by someone who understands what a trades business actually needs. That's the gap I fill.
